δακτύλιος [υ^], Boeot. δακκύλιος (v. δάκτυλος), ὁ,
A.ring, signet, Sapph.35, Hdt.2.38, Ar.Pl.884, Pl.R.359e; ὁ ἐπὶ τοῦ δ. keeper of the signet, LXX To.1.22; “ὁ τῆς πόλεως δ.” OGI229.88 (Smyrna, iii B. C.); “δ. φαρμακίτης” Eup.87, cf. Antiph.177.
II. anything ringshaped, as,
1. felloe of a wheel, Poll.1.145.
2. anus, Dsc.1.70, Luc.Demon.17, PRyl.28.68 (iv A. D.).
3. pl., movable rings on a bit, X.Eq.10.9.
4. stone to which mooring-cables were attached, Hsch.
5. door-handle, Id.
6. end of the steering-paddle, Id.

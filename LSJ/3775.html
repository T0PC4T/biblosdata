II. from resemblance to an ear,
1. handle, esp. of pitchers, cups, etc., “οὔατα δ᾽ αὐτοῦ τέσσαρ᾽ ἔσαν” Il.11.633, cf. 18.378, Bion ap. Plu.2.536a, IG11(2).161 B126 (Delos, iii B. C.), Hero Spir.2.23, Dsc.5.87; [“ποτήριον] ὦτα συντεθλασμένον” Alex.270.3.
2. in Archit., = παρωτίς 4, IG12.372.201, cf. 319.6.
3. οὖς Ἀφροδίτης, a kind of shell-fish, Antig.Car. ap. Ath.3.88a; οὖς θαλάττιον, = ἀγρία λεπάς, Arist.HA529b16.
4. τὰ ὦτα （“οὔατα” Hp.) τῆς καρδίας the auricles of the heart, Hp.Cord.8, Gal.UP6.15, cf. 2.615K.
5. name of part of a bandage, Heraclas ap. Orib.48.15 tit. [Written ὀ̂ς in IG12. Il.cc.] (Cogn. with Lat. auris, Lith. ausìs, OE. éar, etc.; details obscure; nom. sg. οὖς perh. from Ο῎（ϝ）ος (contained in ἀμφώης).)
